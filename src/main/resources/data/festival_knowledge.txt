SECTION 1: FESTIVAL OVERVIEW
-------------------------------------------------------------------

What is SmartFest?
SmartFest is the annual cultural and technical festival of BITS Pilani, held over three days in November. It is one of the largest college festivals in India, attracting over 10,000 participants and visitors from colleges across the country. SmartFest 2025 is the 12th edition of the festival. The theme for SmartFest 2025 is "Convergence" — where culture meets technology.

When is SmartFest 2025?
SmartFest 2025 runs from Friday November 14 to Sunday November 16, 2025. The festival begins at 9:00 AM on Day 1 (November 14) and the closing ceremony is on Day 3 (November 16) at 9:00 PM, followed by the DJ Night.

Where is SmartFest held?
SmartFest is held on the BITS Pilani campus in Rajasthan, India. The campus is spread across 328 acres with multiple venues used simultaneously. All events take place within the campus boundaries.

How many events are there in SmartFest 2025?
SmartFest 2025 has 50 events across 15 categories including Music, Dance, Drama, Debate, Quiz, Tech, Art, Comedy, Literary, Sports, Fashion, Adventure, Entertainment, Wellness, and Cultural.

SECTION 2: REGISTRATION AND ENTRY
-------------------------------------------------------------------

How do I register for SmartFest 2025?
Registration is done online at smartfest.bits.ac.in. External participants must create an account and register as "Visitor." BITS students can use their BITS ID to log in. Most events are free for both BITS students and external participants.

What is the registration deadline?
Online registration closes on November 10, 2025 at 11:59 PM. On-spot registration is available for select open events at the event venue 30 minutes before the event starts.

What are the entry fees?
General entry to the festival campus is free for all visitors. Some individual events have nominal registration fees. Escape Room charges Rs. 100 per person. Pottery Workshop charges Rs. 300 per participant. Photography Walk charges Rs. 200 per participant. All other events are free to participate in or watch.

Are external college students allowed?
Yes, SmartFest welcomes participants from all colleges across India. External participants must carry a valid college ID card for verification at the campus entry gate. Prior online registration is mandatory for external participants.

Can school students attend SmartFest?
School students (Class 10 and above) can attend SmartFest as visitors. They must be accompanied by at least one adult guardian. School students are not eligible to participate in competitive events unless the event specifically mentions school category.

SECTION 5: FESTIVAL WALLET AND PAYMENTS
-------------------------------------------------------------------

What is the SmartFest Wallet?
The SmartFest Wallet is a prepaid digital wallet system used for all transactions within the festival campus. Participants can load money into their wallet and use it for food purchases, merchandise, and paid event registrations. Physical cash is not accepted at most stalls.

How do I set up my SmartFest Wallet?
Your wallet is automatically created when you register at smartfest.bits.ac.in. You can top up your wallet online before the festival or at the Wallet Top-Up counters on campus. Top-up counters are located near the Main Stage, Food Court, and Student Activity Centre entrance.

What are the wallet limits?
Minimum top-up amount: Rs. 100. Maximum wallet balance at any time: Rs. 10,000. Maximum single transaction: Rs. 5,000. If a transaction exceeds Rs. 5,000, it will require additional OTP verification.

What happens to unused wallet balance?
Unused wallet balance can be refunded within 15 days after the festival ends. Submit a refund request at smartfest.bits.ac.in with your registered email and bank details.

Is my wallet balance secure?
Yes. All wallet transactions are monitored by our fraud detection system in real time. Suspicious transactions are flagged and reviewed. You will receive an SMS alert for every transaction above Rs. 500.

SECTION 6: ACCOMMODATION
-------------------------------------------------------------------

Is accommodation available on campus?
Yes. Limited accommodation is available for external participants in the campus hostels. Accommodation must be booked in advance through the registration portal. Availability is on a first-come-first-served basis.

What is the accommodation fee?
Accommodation costs Rs. 500 per person per night for external participants. This includes access to hostel facilities and a morning breakfast. Bed linen is provided.

How many people can share a room?
Hostel rooms are shared accommodation with 4 to 6 beds per room. Room preferences (same college group) can be mentioned during booking but cannot be guaranteed.

Is there separate accommodation for women?
Yes. Girls hostels are separate from boys hostels and have dedicated wardens and security. External female participants are accommodated in the Meera Bhawan girls hostel.

SECTION 7: FOOD AND DINING
-------------------------------------------------------------------

Where can I eat during SmartFest?
The main Food Court operates all three days from 12 PM to 11 PM. Additionally, multiple food stalls are set up across the campus. The campus cafeteria operates from 7 AM to 10 PM serving regular meals.

What types of food are available?
The Food Festival features Indian street food, regional cuisines from across India, continental options, Chinese and Indo-Chinese food, beverages, desserts, and late-night snacks. All dietary preferences including vegetarian, vegan, and Jain food are available.

Are outside food and drinks allowed?
Sealed water bottles and packaged food items are allowed inside the campus. Home-cooked food and open beverages are not permitted inside the venue grounds for hygiene reasons.

Can I pay with cash at food stalls?
Most official SmartFest food stalls accept only the SmartFest Wallet. A few stalls may accept UPI payments. Cash is generally not accepted at official stalls.

SECTION 8: RULES AND CODE OF CONDUCT
-------------------------------------------------------------------

What is the code of conduct at SmartFest?
All participants must behave respectfully toward other participants, volunteers, and performers. Discrimination, harassment, or offensive behaviour of any kind is strictly prohibited. Decisions of judges and event organizers are final.

Are cameras allowed at events?
Personal photography for non-commercial use is allowed at most events. Professional videography equipment (tripods, boom mics, cinema cameras) requires prior permission from the media team. Photography is not allowed backstage or in restricted areas.

Is alcohol allowed on campus?
No. Alcohol and any other intoxicating substances are strictly prohibited on the BITS Pilani campus. Participants found with alcohol will be immediately asked to leave the campus and may be banned from future participation.

What is the dress code?
SmartFest has no specific dress code for general attendees. Event-specific dress codes apply for Fashion Show, Mr and Ms Fest, Classical Dance Showcase (performers must wear classical attire), and Salsa Night (festive attire encouraged).

What are the prohibited items?
Prohibited items include alcohol, drugs, weapons (including toy weapons), loud sound-amplifying devices without permission, and banners or posters with political messaging. Items confiscated at entry will not be returned.

SECTION 9: PRIZES AND RECOGNITION
-------------------------------------------------------------------

What are the prizes at SmartFest?
Prize pools vary by event. Major events like Hackathon 24H (1st prize Rs. 50,000, 2nd Rs. 25,000, 3rd Rs. 10,000), Battle of Bands (1st prize Rs. 30,000 + music production opportunity), and Robotics Challenge (1st Rs. 20,000) have large cash prizes. Most events also award trophies and certificates.

Will I get a participation certificate?
Yes. Digital participation certificates are issued to all registered participants within 7 days after the festival. Certificates are available to download from your SmartFest account portal.

Are there internship opportunities?
Yes. Hackathon 24H and App Development Contest winners are offered internship interviews with sponsor companies including TechCorp Solutions, InnovateLabs, and StartupNest. Business Quiz winners are offered internship opportunities with FinanceFirst consulting.

SECTION 10: VOLUNTEERING AND ORGANIZING
-------------------------------------------------------------------

How can I volunteer at SmartFest?
BITS Pilani students can apply to volunteer through the internal volunteer portal. Volunteers get priority access to all events, a festival kit including T-shirt, and a certificate of volunteering. Applications are open until November 1, 2025.

Who organizes SmartFest?
SmartFest is organized by the Student Cultural Council of BITS Pilani. Each event has a dedicated student coordinator team. The overall festival is supervised by the Dean of Student Welfare.

SECTION 11: TRANSPORT AND CONNECTIVITY
-------------------------------------------------------------------

How do I reach BITS Pilani campus?
BITS Pilani is located in Pilani town, Jhunjhunu district, Rajasthan. Nearest railway station is Loharu (45 km) or Rewari (70 km). Nearest airport is Indira Gandhi International Airport, New Delhi (220 km). Special festival buses will operate from Loharu station and Jhunjhunu bus stand every 2 hours from November 13 to November 17.

Is there WiFi on campus during SmartFest?
Yes. SmartFest provides free WiFi access to all registered participants during the festival. Connect to the network SmartFest_Guest and use your registered mobile number to authenticate.

Is there parking available?
Yes. Vehicle parking is available in the designated parking areas near the Main Gate and the Sports Complex gate. Two-wheelers and four-wheelers have separate parking zones. Parking is free.

SECTION 12: EMERGENCY AND SAFETY
-------------------------------------------------------------------

Is there a medical facility on campus?
Yes. The BITS Pilani Medical Centre operates 24 hours during the festival. First aid posts are located at the Main Stage, Food Court, and Sports Complex. Ambulance is on standby throughout the festival.

What do I do in case of an emergency?
Call the SmartFest Emergency Helpline: 1800-SMARTFEST (toll-free). You can also approach any volunteer or security personnel (identifiable by their orange vests).

Is the campus safe?
Yes. The campus has CCTV surveillance and security personnel stationed at all major venues and campus entry points. Female participants can contact the Women's Safety Helpdesk located at the Student Activity Centre.
